<div class="page">
  <h2>Reflection Capacity</h2>
  <div class="rule"></div>

  <div class="card">
    <p style="margin:0;">
      Every person carries the image of God, expressed through four reflections: Architect,
      Artisan, Shepherd, and Steward. But not every reflection carries the same range. Your
      design capacity describes how strongly God’s grace is currently flowing through each
      part of your divine design. It does not measure effort or ability but reveals where grace
      feels natural, steady, stretched, or strained.
    </p>

    <p style="margin:10px 0 0;">
      In other words, capacity shows how God’s power moves through you; what gives life,
      what requires faith, and what invites rest. When your capacity is Abundant, you feel
      joy and ease; when it is Steady, you serve faithfully; when it is Limited, you depend
      intentionally on God; and when it is Drained, you learn surrender and partnership.
      Design capacity reminds us that His grace is always sufficient.
    </p>

    <p style="margin:10px 0 0; font-weight:700;">
      Each one should use whatever gift you have received to serve others, as faithful
      stewards of God’s grace in its various forms. (1 Peter 4:10)
    </p>

    <h3 style="margin-top:14px;">The Purpose of Capacity</h3>
    <ul>
      <li><strong>Reveal natural grace flow:</strong> What comes easily, joyfully, and fruitfully.</li>
      <li><strong>Expose overused or undernourished energies:</strong> Where strain or fatigue may occur.</li>
      <li><strong>Support development and discipleship:</strong> Guiding self-awareness, leadership growth, and spiritual balance.</li>
      <li><strong>Honor divine diversity:</strong> Reminding us that no design is superior; each simply reflects a different grace intensity.</li>
    </ul>

    <h3 style="margin-top:14px;">Capacity mirrors God’s relational approach with His people:</h3>
    <ul>
