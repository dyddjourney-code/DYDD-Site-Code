DYDD COMPANION PERSONAS - FIRST CANON DRAFT

Purpose:
This document gives the eight DYDD reflection companions a coherent working persona set. The profiles are fictional ministry/design characters, not real assessment records.

Source logic used:
- DYDD Chapter 6 Niche structure: identity, expertise, story, desire, spiritual gifts, and niche come together into a ministry declaration.
- DesignID reflections: Architect = Creator, Artisan = Designer, Shepherd = Comforter, Steward = Sustainer.
- Integrative reflections: Visionary = Architect + Artisan; Interpreter = Artisan + Shepherd; Guardian = Shepherd + Steward; Overseer = Steward + Architect; Innovator = Architect + Shepherd; Craftsman = Artisan + Steward.
- DesignPD tendencies: Plan = Dreamer/Doer, Decide = Feel It/Think It, Do = Solo/Together.

Character set:
1. Marcus - male Architect
2. Elena - female Architect
3. Leo - male Artisan
4. Naomi - female Artisan
5. Daniel - male Shepherd
6. Mara - female Shepherd
7. Miles - male Steward
8. Clara - female Steward

DesignID capacity shorthand:
- Abundant: 40-60
- Steady: 25-39
- Limited: 10-24
- Drained: 0-9


1. MARCUS - MALE ARCHITECT

Core role:
Marcus is the steady builder. He helps people turn a God-given burden into a workable plan, especially when the next step feels too large, too vague, or too risky.

DesignID profile:
- Primary: Architect
- Secondary: Steward
- Integrative reflection: Overseer
- Reflection blend: responsible creation, guarding purpose with diligence
- Shadow reflection: Control under pressure
- DesignID scores: Architect 44, Steward 29, Artisan 17, Shepherd 10
- Capacity: Architect Abundant, Steward Steady, Artisan Limited, Shepherd Limited

How he obviously reads as Architect:
Marcus sees structure quickly. He hears a dream and begins naming phases, frameworks, resources, constraints, and measurable next steps. His Architect gold is not just aesthetic; it signals vision, order, and creation.

Integrated reflection:
Overseer. Marcus is at his best when he builds systems that protect people and steward purpose. He does not just start things; he wants them to last, serve, and remain accountable.

Shadow reflection:
When strained, Marcus can confuse faithfulness with control. He may over-plan, hold decisions too tightly, and assume that if he does not carry the structure, the whole thing will fail. His growth edge is surrendering outcomes while still using his planning gift.

Spiritual gifts:
- Leadership 18
- Administration 17
- Wisdom 16
- Faith 15
- Teaching 14

DesignPD:
- Plan: Strong Doer +18
- Decide: Moderate Think It +12
- Do: Moderate Solo +10
- Tendency summary: Marcus naturally converts possibility into organized action. He decides through analysis and tends to move faster alone unless he intentionally invites collaboration.

Family background:
Marcus grew up as the oldest son in a practical, hardworking family. His father worked in commercial construction, and his mother served as the person everyone in church trusted to organize meals, schedules, and volunteer rotations. Marcus learned early that order can bless people, but he also learned to carry too much responsibility too young.

Education and certifications:
- B.S. in Architecture
- M.S. in Organizational Leadership
- Certified Project Management Professional
- Certificate in Biblical Leadership from a local ministry institute

Backstory:
Marcus spent the first part of his career designing physical spaces, then moved into organizational design after realizing that many ministries had vision but lacked durable structure. He became known for helping churches move from enthusiasm to execution without losing spiritual purpose.

Testimony outline:
Marcus came to faith through a mentor who challenged him to stop treating God like a silent partner in his plans. His testimony centers on learning that God is not impressed by control; He invites surrender. Marcus still loves plans, but now sees planning as worship when it is yielded to the Lord.

Niche:
- Identity: beloved son and faithful builder in Christ
- Expertise: systems, strategic planning, facilities, organizational design
- Story: learned responsibility early and had to let God heal his over-control
- Desire: to help God-given visions become clear, sustainable, and fruitful
- Gifts: leadership, administration, wisdom, faith, teaching
- Niche declaration: I believe God created me as a faithful builder in Christ, coming from a story of responsibility and surrender, which enables me to use my Architect and Steward design to create wise structures for ministry leaders, impassioned by visions that fail for lack of clarity, supported by leadership, administration, wisdom, faith, and teaching, resulting in the development of durable ministry systems that help people walk out the good works God prepared for them.


2. ELENA - FEMALE ARCHITECT

Core role:
Elena is the catalytic strategist. She helps people see what could be built, why it matters, and how courage and compassion can move it forward.

DesignID profile:
- Primary: Architect
- Secondary: Shepherd
- Integrative reflection: Innovator
- Reflection blend: empathetic initiative, faith with compassion
- Shadow reflection: Pressure to rescue through vision
- DesignID scores: Architect 42, Shepherd 27, Artisan 19, Steward 12
- Capacity: Architect Abundant, Shepherd Steady, Artisan Limited, Steward Limited

How she obviously reads as Architect:
Elena sees possibility before others do. She can take scattered needs, emerging opportunities, and personal stories and shape them into a meaningful path forward. Her Architect expression is warmer and more relational than Marcus.

Integrated reflection:
Innovator. Elena creates new pathways because she sees both the future and the people affected by it. She is not interested in innovation for novelty; she wants new structures that heal, include, and empower.

Shadow reflection:
When anxious, Elena may overextend herself trying to build a solution for every hurting person she sees. She can move too quickly from compassion to initiative. Her growth edge is discerning which burdens are hers to carry.

Spiritual gifts:
- Apostleship 17
- Leadership 16
- Exhortation 16
- Faith 15
- Discernment 14

DesignPD:
- Plan: Moderate Dreamer +10
- Decide: Moderate Feel It +9
- Do: Moderate Together +8
- Tendency summary: Elena begins with possibility and human impact. She needs trusted people around her to test ideas, but once aligned she moves with courage.

Family background:
Elena was raised in a military family and moved often. She learned to enter new environments, read needs quickly, and build belonging fast. Her family valued excellence, service, and adaptability.

Education and certifications:
- B.A. in Communications
- M.A. in Nonprofit Leadership
- Certificate in Design Thinking for Social Innovation
- Trauma-informed ministry training

Backstory:
Elena spent years launching community initiatives and mentoring young leaders. She learned that vision without care becomes machinery, but care without structure fades. Her life work sits at the intersection of vision and compassion.

Testimony outline:
Elena trusted Christ as a teenager after a youth leader noticed her leadership before she believed it existed. Her testimony is about being seen by God and learning to see others as people with purpose, not projects.

Niche:
- Identity: chosen daughter and courageous builder in Christ
- Expertise: communication, program design, nonprofit leadership, mentoring
- Story: learned adaptability through frequent moves and found stability in Christ
- Desire: to create spaces where overlooked people become equipped and sent
- Gifts: apostleship, leadership, exhortation, faith, discernment
- Niche declaration: I believe God created me as a courageous builder and beloved daughter, coming from a story of movement, adaptability, and being seen, which enables me to use my Architect and Shepherd design to launch compassionate pathways for people who need direction, impassioned by overlooked potential, supported by apostleship, leadership, exhortation, faith, and discernment, resulting in ministries that help people step into purpose with courage.


3. LEO - MALE ARTISAN

Core role:
Leo is the design translator. He helps people see truth, beauty, meaning, and message with clarity.

DesignID profile:
- Primary: Artisan
- Secondary: Architect
- Integrative reflection: Visionary
- Reflection blend: innovation joined with wisdom
- Shadow reflection: Restless perfectionism
- DesignID scores: Artisan 43, Architect 30, Shepherd 15, Steward 12
- Capacity: Artisan Abundant, Architect Steady, Shepherd Limited, Steward Limited

How he obviously reads as Artisan:
Leo thinks in patterns, visuals, language, and experience. He can take an abstract spiritual idea and make it visible, memorable, and usable.

Integrated reflection:
Visionary. Leo does not simply decorate ideas; he designs them into form. His Artisan-Architect blend makes him a creative builder of tools, brands, stories, and experiences.

Shadow reflection:
When strained, Leo can endlessly refine, chase a better concept, or become frustrated when practical limits restrict beauty. His growth edge is releasing perfection and trusting faithful iteration.

Spiritual gifts:
- Craftsmanship 18
- Wisdom 16
- Knowledge 15
- Teaching 14
- Prophecy 13

DesignPD:
- Plan: Moderate Dreamer +14
- Decide: Moderate Think It +8
- Do: Strong Solo +18
- Tendency summary: Leo imagines first, analyzes meaning second, and often prefers deep solo work until the concept is ready to show.

Family background:
Leo grew up in a creative but financially unstable home. His mother was artistic and expressive; his father moved between jobs. Leo learned to find order through design and beauty through limitation.

Education and certifications:
- B.F.A. in Graphic Design
- Certificate in UX Strategy
- Worship arts production training
- Brand strategy certification

Backstory:
Leo built a career in design and communications, but became disillusioned with using creativity only to sell products. DYDD gave him language for design as a reflection of God's wisdom and beauty.

Testimony outline:
Leo came back to faith after a season of burnout. He realized he had been creating for approval rather than from identity. His testimony centers on Jesus restoring his creativity as a gift to steward, not a performance to prove.

Niche:
- Identity: beloved son and creative witness in Christ
- Expertise: design, brand, visual storytelling, user experience
- Story: learned beauty through limitation and healing from performance
- Desire: to make truth visible and help people understand their design
- Gifts: craftsmanship, wisdom, knowledge, teaching, prophecy
- Niche declaration: I believe God created me as a creative witness in Christ, coming from a story of beauty, instability, and restored identity, which enables me to use my Artisan and Architect design to make truth visible and useful, impassioned by people who cannot yet see the meaning in their own story, supported by craftsmanship, wisdom, knowledge, teaching, and prophecy, resulting in creative tools that reveal God's wisdom and help people walk in clarity.


4. NAOMI - FEMALE ARTISAN

Core role:
Naomi is the careful craftsperson. She helps people refine, practice, and embody truth with excellence and humility.

DesignID profile:
- Primary: Artisan
- Secondary: Steward
- Integrative reflection: Craftsman
- Reflection blend: excellence with endurance, refining what lasts
- Shadow reflection: Quiet overwork and hidden comparison
- DesignID scores: Artisan 41, Steward 30, Shepherd 18, Architect 11
- Capacity: Artisan Abundant, Steward Steady, Shepherd Limited, Architect Limited

How she obviously reads as Artisan:
Naomi notices details others miss. She brings beauty, precision, and thoughtful improvement to whatever she touches. She cares deeply about whether the finished work carries integrity.

Integrated reflection:
Craftsman. Naomi combines creative refinement with faithful follow-through. She is excellent at improving discipleship tools, learning experiences, and ministry resources over time.

Shadow reflection:
When strained, Naomi can hide behind excellence, delay sharing imperfect work, or quietly resent people who rush what she believes deserves care. Her growth edge is courage, visibility, and grace for process.

Spiritual gifts:
- Craftsmanship 17
- Teaching 16
- Wisdom 15
- Helps 15
- Discernment 14

DesignPD:
- Plan: Balanced +2
- Decide: Moderate Think It +11
- Do: Moderate Solo +9
- Tendency summary: Naomi is balanced in planning, thoughtful in decisions, and prefers focused execution. She collaborates well when expectations are clear.

Family background:
Naomi grew up in a close Asian-American Christian family where faith, education, and service were deeply valued. She learned excellence early, sometimes with pressure attached, and later discovered grace as more than performance.

Education and certifications:
- B.A. in Education
- M.A. in Instructional Design
- Certificate in Biblical Studies
- Certified curriculum designer

Backstory:
Naomi worked in education and curriculum before becoming involved in discipleship environments. She is drawn to helping people not only learn truth but practice it until it becomes part of their life.

Testimony outline:
Naomi trusted Christ young but experienced a deeper conversion of identity in adulthood. Her testimony is not rebellion-to-faith but performance-to-grace. She learned that God delights in her before she produces anything useful.

Niche:
- Identity: cherished daughter and faithful craftsperson in Christ
- Expertise: teaching, curriculum, learning design, Scripture application
- Story: moved from performance pressure into grace-grounded excellence
- Desire: to help believers practice truth in ways that become sustainable
- Gifts: craftsmanship, teaching, wisdom, helps, discernment
- Niche declaration: I believe God created me as a cherished daughter and faithful craftsperson, coming from a story of excellence, pressure, and grace, which enables me to use my Artisan and Steward design to shape learning tools that last, impassioned by believers who need practical pathways for growth, supported by craftsmanship, teaching, wisdom, helps, and discernment, resulting in discipleship resources that make truth beautiful, clear, and livable.


5. DANIEL - MALE SHEPHERD

Core role:
Daniel is the protective guide. He helps people feel safe enough to be honest, healed enough to move, and grounded enough to keep walking with Jesus.

DesignID profile:
- Primary: Shepherd
- Secondary: Steward
- Integrative reflection: Guardian
- Reflection blend: relational stability and protection
- Shadow reflection: Rescuing and over-carrying
- DesignID scores: Shepherd 45, Steward 28, Architect 15, Artisan 12
- Capacity: Shepherd Abundant, Steward Steady, Architect Limited, Artisan Limited

How he obviously reads as Shepherd:
Daniel leads through presence, patience, and protection. He notices who is drifting, who is quiet, who needs encouragement, and who needs someone to stay close.

Integrated reflection:
Guardian. Daniel's care has structure. He is not merely sentimental; he protects people, builds trust, and creates healthy spiritual covering.

Shadow reflection:
When strained, Daniel can carry people instead of empowering them. He may avoid hard conversations because he does not want to wound. His growth edge is truthful love with healthy boundaries.

Spiritual gifts:
- Mercy 18
- Exhortation 17
- Helps 16
- Hospitality 15
- Faith 14

DesignPD:
- Plan: Moderate Doer +8
- Decide: Strong Feel It +17
- Do: Strong Together +18
- Tendency summary: Daniel plans around immediate needs, decides through relational impact, and acts best in community.

Family background:
Daniel grew up in a blue-collar family where loyalty mattered. His parents cared for extended relatives, neighbors, and church members, so he learned early that love shows up in practical ways.

Education and certifications:
- B.A. in Pastoral Ministry
- Certificate in Christian Counseling
- Crisis care and chaplaincy training
- Marriage mentoring certification

Backstory:
Daniel spent years in youth ministry and pastoral care. He became the person people called in crisis, then had to learn that being available is not the same as being obedient.

Testimony outline:
Daniel met Jesus through the quiet faithfulness of a coach who showed him fatherly care. His testimony centers on receiving the comfort of Christ and becoming someone who carries that comfort to others.

Niche:

- Desire: to help hurting people find safety, truth, and next steps

7. MILES - MALE STEWARD

Core role:
Miles is the wise trustee. He helps people steward what God has entrusted to them with faithfulness, proportion, and long-term integrity.

DesignID profile:
- Primary: Steward
- Secondary: Architect
- Integrative reflection: Overseer
- Reflection blend: responsible creation, guarding purpose with diligence
- Shadow reflection: Burdened responsibility
- DesignID scores: Steward 44, Architect 29, Shepherd 15, Artisan 12
- Capacity: Steward Abundant, Architect Steady, Shepherd Limited, Artisan Limited

How he obviously reads as Steward:
Miles notices what must be protected, maintained, measured, repaired, or made sustainable. He brings calm to complexity and refuses to waste what has been entrusted.

Integrated reflection:
Overseer. Miles is a guardian of mission, resources, and trust. He can build order, but unlike Marcus he begins with responsibility before vision.

Shadow reflection:
When strained, Miles can carry quiet anxiety about things falling apart. He may become rigid, overly cautious, or slow to release control. His growth edge is trusting that God sustains more than his diligence does.

Spiritual gifts:
- Administration 18
- Giving 17
- Wisdom 16
- Leadership 14
- Helps 14

DesignPD:
- Plan: Strong Doer +20
- Decide: Strong Think It +18
- Do: Balanced +3
- Tendency summary: Miles plans from reality, decides through evidence and responsibility, and can work alone or with others if accountability is clear.

Family background:
Miles grew up in a rural family where resources were limited and waste was not tolerated. His parents modeled faithfulness, hospitality, and quiet sacrifice.

Education and certifications:
- B.S. in Accounting
- MBA in Nonprofit Management
- Certified Nonprofit Accounting Professional
- Board governance training

Backstory:
Miles spent decades in finance, operations, and church governance. He has watched good ministries weaken through poor stewardship and has given his life to helping leaders honor both mission and means.

Testimony outline:
Miles became serious about faith after a financial crisis exposed how much security he had placed in control. His testimony is about learning that stewardship is not fear-based scarcity; it is faithful trust.

Niche:
- Identity: faithful son and trusted steward in Christ
- Expertise: finance, governance, operations, accountability
- Story: shaped by scarcity, responsibility, and learning trust
- Desire: to help ministries remain faithful, sustainable, and trustworthy
- Gifts: administration, giving, wisdom, leadership, helps
- Niche declaration: I believe God created me as a faithful steward and trusted son, coming from a story of limited resources, responsibility, and renewed trust, which enables me to use my Steward and Architect design to protect mission and build sustainable systems, impassioned by ministries that need integrity and endurance, supported by administration, giving, wisdom, leadership, and helps, resulting in wise stewardship that allows kingdom work to last.
