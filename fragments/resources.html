What Is DesignPD?

A practical next step after DesignID

DesignID helps you understand the way God’s design is reflected through you. It gives language to identity, reflection, and contribution. DesignPD takes the next step by helping you understand how that design tends to move in everyday life.

The PD in DesignPD can be understood as both Personal Development and Professional Development. The same tendencies that shape how you approach planning, decisions, and action at work also shape how you lead, relate, serve, and carry responsibility in the rest of life.

In that sense, DesignPD is where design becomes practical. It helps you see how your inner wiring becomes visible in your real patterns, especially when expectations rise, pressure builds, or collaboration becomes complex.

The Three Core Movements

Plan, Decide, and Do

DesignPD centers around three simple but powerful movements. Every person engages in all three, but each person tends to approach them a little differently. These patterns shape how you prepare, process, and move.

PLAN

How you approach vision and execution

Dreamer
Doer

The Plan axis looks at how you naturally approach preparation, momentum, and forward movement. Some people begin with imagination, possibilities, and future vision. Others begin with action, structure, and practical next steps.

Neither side is better. Dreamers help open possibilities. Doers help bring movement and traction. DesignPD helps you understand where you tend to begin and how that tendency affects the way you lead, prepare, and contribute.

Why it matters: Your planning tendency affects how you handle goals, priorities, meetings, change, and the pace at which ideas become action.
DECIDE

How you process choices and make decisions

Feel It
Think It

The Decide axis looks at how you tend to process information when choices need to be made. Some people lead with instinct, empathy, and internal awareness. Others lead with analysis, objectivity, and careful evaluation.

Healthy decisions need both discernment and clarity. DesignPD helps reveal where you naturally start, which often explains why certain decisions feel obvious to you while others require more effort, more input, or more time.

Why it matters: Your decision tendency affects how you weigh options, handle tension, respond to people, and navigate moments where conviction and complexity meet.
DO

How you move work forward

Solo
Together

The Do axis looks at how you tend to carry responsibility and complete what is in front of you. Some people gain momentum through independent ownership. Others gain momentum through shared effort and collaboration.

Both styles bring value. Solo tendencies often create speed and ownership. Together tendencies often create shared clarity, stronger buy in, and broader engagement. DesignPD helps make those patterns visible so they can become strengths instead of friction points.

Why it matters: Your doing tendency affects communication, delegation, follow through, accountability, and the way you contribute inside teams and relationships.

Frequently Asked Questions

Questions about DesignPD

A few helpful answers before you begin your DesignPD report.

Do I need to complete DesignID before DesignPD?

Yes. DesignPD is designed to build on the foundation of DesignID. DesignID helps you understand your design, while DesignPD helps you understand how that design tends to show up in planning, decision making, and action.

What does DesignPD measure?

DesignPD explores how you naturally Plan, Decide, and Do. It looks at tendencies such as vision and execution, intuition and analysis, and independent or collaborative action.

Is DesignPD for personal development or professional development?

It can support both. DesignPD helps individuals grow in self-awareness while also helping leaders and teams understand how people work, communicate, decide, and contribute together.

How can teams use DesignPD?

Teams can use DesignPD to better understand differences in planning, decision making, collaboration, leadership, pressure, and sustainability. This can reduce misunderstanding and support healthier alignment.

Is DesignPD a personality test?

No. DesignPD is not meant to label or limit you. It is a reflection tool that helps you recognize patterns in how your design tends to move into everyday action.

Ready to move from understanding your design to applying it?

If you have already completed DesignID, DesignPD is your next step. Discover how you naturally plan, make decisions, and move work forward so you can live and lead with greater clarity.
