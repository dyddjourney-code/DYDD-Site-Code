DesignID Overview

DesignID™ is a faith-integrated assessment that measures how individuals perceive how they reflect who God is. The four reflections used in this assessment are titled Architect, Artisan, Shepherd, and Steward. Each represents a reflection of how God works through human beings. Together, they help us better understand the following aspects of God: The Creator, Designer, Comforter, and Sustainer. The DesignID Assessment quantifies an individual’s preferences across these four aspects.

Vision: To help every believer discover, develop, and deploy their God-given design, equipping the Church to flourish through unity, diversity, and purpose.

DYDD + DesignID gives individuals a tangible way to live out the DYDD anchor verse:
“For we are God’s handiwork, created in Christ Jesus to do good works, which God prepared in advance for us to do.” (Ephesians 2:10)

Biblical Foundation

  • Genesis 1:27 — We were created to reflect who God is.
  • 1 Peter 2:9 — Identity flows from whose we are, not who we are.
  • 1 Corinthians 15:10 — Grace does not just forgive; it fuels, sustains, and directs.
  • Galatians 5:16–17 — We are to operate in grace, not selfishness.

DesignID operationalizes these biblical truths into a practical assessment that quantifies how individuals reflect God’s image through creating (Architect), designing (Artisan), comforting (Shepherd), and sustaining (Steward).

Reflection Capacity

Every person carries the image of God, expressed through four reflections: Architect, Artisan, Shepherd, and Steward. But not every reflection carries the same range. Your design capacity describes how strongly God’s grace is currently flowing through each part of your divine design. It does not measure effort or ability but reveals where grace feels natural, steady, stretched, or strained.

In other words, capacity shows how God’s power moves through you; what gives life, what requires faith, and what invites rest. When your capacity is Abundant, you feel joy and ease; when it is Steady, you serve faithfully; when it is Limited, you depend intentionally on God; and when it is Drained, you learn surrender and partnership. Design capacity reminds us that His grace is always sufficient.

Each one should use whatever gift you have received to serve others, as faithful stewards of God’s grace in its various forms. (1 Peter 4:10)

The Purpose of Capacity

  • Reveal natural grace flow: What comes easily, joyfully, and fruitfully.
  • Expose overused or undernourished energies: Where strain or fatigue may occur.
  • Support development and discipleship: Guiding self-awareness, leadership growth, and spiritual balance.
  • Honor divine diversity: Reminding us that no design is superior; each simply reflects a different grace intensity.

Capacity mirrors God’s relational approach with His people:

  • He gives Abundant grace to empower calling.
  • He sustains with Steady grace for endurance.
  • He teaches through Limited grace to cultivate dependence.
  • He redeems Drained grace by inviting rest and partnership.

Shadow Reflection

Shadow reflections describe what happens when our divine design moves out of alignment with grace. They are not sin themselves, but rather the distractions, distortions, or divided attention that pull our reflection of God away from its intended purpose. When grace flows freely, we reflect His image clearly, but when pride, fear, or self-reliance interrupt that flow, our reflection bends toward self instead of service.

Shadows appear when the gift meant to bless becomes a burden we try to carry alone. Without awareness, these unguarded tendencies can mature into sin; control instead of courage, perfectionism instead of pursuit of excellence, people-pleasing instead of love.

Scripture reminds us, “If we claim to have fellowship with him and yet walk in the darkness, we lie and do not live out the truth” (1 John 1:6). The purpose of identifying shadow reflections is therefore not condemnation but restoration, to recognize where light has dimmed. As Psalm 139:23–24 prays, “Search me, God, and know my heart…See if there is any offensive way in me, and lead me in the way everlasting.”

Discover Your Divine Design is not another self-help formula. It is an invitation to uncover the truth of who you are and how you are made. This journey does not begin with what you do. It begins with who you are.

Discover Your Divine Design is a faith-based development program designed to help individuals and groups explore how God has uniquely created them to live with purpose.
