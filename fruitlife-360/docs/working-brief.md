# FruitLife 360 Working Brief

Created: 2026-07-05

## Purpose

FruitLife 360 is a Galatians 5 fruit of the Spirit assessment/report experience for Discover Your Divine Design.

The goal is not to tell people whether they are "good" or "bad" at a fruit of the Spirit. The goal is to help them see:

- which fruit is most consistently visible in their life right now
- which fruit is steady and developing
- which fruit may be a current growth invitation from the Holy Spirit
- how the fruit may express through their God-given design when paired with DesignID

Core framing:

> This assessment is not measuring whether the fruit of the Spirit is present or absent in your life. In Christ, this fruit is being formed in you. You are identifying which expressions are most visible, most practiced, and most ready for growth in this season.

## Key Decisions From 2026-07-05

- Use the full three-part structure: rank, consistency, and pressure.
- Keep drag-and-drop ranking as the main report driver.
- Add rating questions to measure intensity, so ranking does not overstate weakness.
- Use "visible," "consistent," "developing," and "growth invitation" language.
- Avoid "least skilled," "bad at," "low fruit," or holiness-grading language.
- Build a complete standalone FruitLife report first.
- Add DesignID as an optional enrichment layer, not a requirement.
- Prepare the structure so it can later support self-assessment and peer/360 comparison, similar to the Developing People Group annual review.

## Theological And Tone Guardrails

FruitLife 360 should feel honoring, spiritually grounded, and credible.

Use language like:

- most visible fruit
- consistently visible
- steady developing fruit
- current growth invitation
- under pressure
- season of formation
- evidence of the Spirit's work

Avoid language like:

- weak fruit
- low score means deficient
- bad at patience
- least spiritual
- failed area
- holiness score

Important distinction:

- The assessment observes expression and formation.
- It does not judge salvation, worth, spiritual standing, or God's love for the person.

## Assessment Structure

### Part 1: Drag-And-Drop Ranking

Prompt:

> Rank these nine expressions of the fruit of the Spirit from most consistently visible in your life right now to least consistently visible in this current season.

Recommended report interpretation:

- Top 3: most visible fruit
- Middle 3: steady developing fruit
- Bottom 3: current growth invitation fruit

Important: Bottom-ranked fruit should never be described as absent. It is "less visible relative to the others."

### Part 2: Consistency Ratings

Purpose: Measure how strongly each fruit is visible overall.

Core question:

> How consistently do others experience this fruit from you?

Suggested scale:

1. Rarely visible right now
2. Occasionally visible
3. Somewhat consistent
4. Usually consistent
5. Deeply consistent

### Part 3: Pressure Ratings

Purpose: Measure how the fruit shows up under stress, conflict, delay, uncertainty, temptation, or disappointment.

Core question:

> How naturally does this fruit show up when life is pressured?

Suggested scale:

1. Rarely visible under pressure
2. Occasionally visible under pressure
3. Somewhat steady under pressure
4. Usually steady under pressure
5. Deeply steady under pressure

### Optional Final Reflection

Prompt:

> Which fruit do you most desire the Holy Spirit to grow in you during this season?

This gives the report a discipleship direction rather than only a profile result.

## Fruit-Specific Pressure Statements

These can be used as the pressure rating wording if the form needs more specific statements instead of the generic pressure question.

- Love: When someone is difficult to love, I still move toward their good.
- Joy: My inner steadiness is not easily taken by circumstances.
- Peace: I can remain settled even when outcomes are uncertain.
- Patience: I give people and processes room to grow without forcing control.
- Kindness: I notice practical ways to care for people and act on them.
- Goodness: I choose what is right even when it costs me.
- Faithfulness: People can count on me to follow through over time.
- Gentleness: I can bring strength without harshness.
- Self-control: My desires and reactions do not regularly rule my decisions.

## Scoring Model

Use three inputs per fruit:

1. Rank position
2. Consistency score
3. Pressure score

The ranking gives relative order. The ratings give intensity.

Example interpretation logic:

- Ranked high + high consistency + high pressure: signature visible fruit
- Ranked high + high consistency + low pressure: visible fruit with pressure vulnerability
- Ranked low + high consistency: present and meaningful, but less defining relative to other fruit
- Ranked low + low consistency + low pressure: likely growth invitation
- Ranked middle + balanced scores: steady developing fruit

Recommended principle:

> Rank determines placement in the report. Consistency and pressure determine tone, nuance, and coaching language.

## Report Modes

### Mode 1: Standalone FruitLife Report

Works for anyone, even if they have never taken DesignID.

Inputs:

- Fruit ranking
- Consistency ratings
- Pressure ratings
- Optional final reflection
- Optional peer/360 ratings in a later version

This version must feel complete, valuable, and spiritually meaningful by itself.

### Mode 2: FruitLife + DesignID Integrated Report

Uses all FruitLife data, then interprets the fruit through the person's DesignID type.

Core principle:

> FruitLife measures spiritual fruit. DesignID explains the person's natural expression channel.

DesignID should not change the fruit score. It should change how the report describes, coaches, and applies the fruit.

Example: Peace is highly visible.

- Shepherd expression: calming people relationally
- Architect expression: bringing clarity and order
- Artisan expression: creating beauty, hope, and emotional atmosphere
- Steward expression: stabilizing life through dependability and provision

## DesignID Intake Logic

Ask:

> Have you completed your DesignID assessment?

Options:

- Yes, I know my DesignID
- Yes, but I am not sure of my result
- No, not yet

If they know it, ask:

> Which DesignID best reflects you?

Options:

- Shepherd
- Architect
- Artisan
- Steward
- I am not sure

Recommended fallback language:

> No problem. Your FruitLife 360 report will still be complete. If you complete your DesignID later, you can unlock an added layer showing how your fruit may uniquely express through your design.

### DesignID Match Priority

If technically possible, match by email against the existing DesignID spreadsheet.

Data source priority:

1. Email match from the DesignID spreadsheet: strongest
2. Self-selected DesignID: useful but less verified
3. No DesignID: standalone FruitLife report

DesignID should be treated as optional enrichment, not a gate.

## 360 / Peer Review Direction

John intends FruitLife 360 to eventually work like the Developing People Group annual review:

- self-assessment only
- or self-assessment plus peer/observer feedback
- comparative structure between self-view and how others experience the person

Likely future structure:

1. Setup form creates the assessment event.
2. Participant completes self-assessment.
3. Participant invites peers/observers.
4. Peers rate visible consistency and pressure expression.
5. Report compares self-view to peer view.
6. Report gives encouragement, insight, and growth invitations.

For the base version, prioritize the self-assessment and report engine first. Keep field names and workbook tabs compatible with a future peer layer.

## Example Report Language

For a lower-ranked fruit with decent scores:

> Patience ranked lower in your profile, but your consistency score suggests it is still meaningfully present. This may not be an absence of patience, but an invitation to let patience become more visible when pressure increases.

For the DesignID layer:

> Because your DesignID appears to be Architect, this fruit may express through clarity, strategy, wise structure, and problem-solving. This does not change your FruitLife result; it helps explain how the fruit may naturally become visible through the way God designed you.
